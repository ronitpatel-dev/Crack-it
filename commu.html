💬 "Speak Smart – Crack Interviews with Confidence"


🗣️ 1. Verbal Communication
Spoken words, tone, and clarity.
Used in face-to-face interviews, group discussions, or phone calls.
Tips: Be clear, concise, confident, and maintain a moderate pace.

✍️ 2. Non-Verbal Communication
Body language, facial expressions, posture, gestures, and eye contact.
Often observed silently by interviewers to assess confidence and attitude.
Tips: Maintain eye contact, sit straight, avoid fidgeting, and smile naturally.

📄 3. Written Communication
Seen in resumes, cover letters, emails, and online applications.
Demonstrates grammar, structure, and professionalism.
Tips: Proofread carefully, be formal but not robotic, and keep it relevant.

💬 4. Listening Skills (Active Communication)
Shows your ability to understand and respond appropriately.
Important in team roles and client interaction.
Tips: Nod when appropriate, don’t interrupt, and respond thoughtfully.

🌐 5. Digital Communication
Communication through emails, chats, or video calls (especially post-COVID).
Common in remote interviews or tech roles.
Tips: Ensure good connectivity, dress properly even for virtual calls, and speak clearly.
